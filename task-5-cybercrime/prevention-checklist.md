1.	Verify UPI "Collect Requests"

Before entering your PIN, check if the transaction is for sending or receiving money. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" on apps like Google Pay or PhonePe, tricking you into thinking you are receiving a refund or prize when you are actually authorizing a payment from your account.

2.	No PIN/OTP for Receiving Money

Remember the golden rule: You never need to enter your UPI PIN or share an OTP to receive money into your account. If someone asks for your PIN to "credit" a scholarship, prize, or payment, it is a scam.

3.	Beware of Fake Internship/Job Offers

Be skeptical of high-paying "work-from-home" or "task-based" jobs (e.g., liking YouTube videos or rating hotels on Telegram). Legitimate companies will never ask for a "security deposit," "registration fee," or "laptop charges" before you start working.

4.	Use "Safe Words" Against AI Voice Clones

With the rise of AI voice cloning, scammers can mimic a friend or parent's voice to claim an emergency. Establish a private family safe word to verify identities during urgent "accident" or "arrest" calls.

5.	Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking

Never use open Wi-Fi in cafes, hostels, or railway stations for sensitive tasks like logging into your bank portal or making UPI payments. These networks are easily intercepted by "eavesdropping" attacks.

6.	Secure Your Digital Identity

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all social media and email accounts. Use a dedicated app-based authenticator (like Google Authenticator) rather than SMS, as hackers can use "SIM swap" techniques to intercept your text messages.

7.	Identify "Digital Arrest" Scams

If you receive a video call from someone claiming to be from the police, CBI, or customs (often showing fake IDs or warrants) and they demand you stay on the call under "digital arrest," hang up immediately. Real Indian law enforcement agencies do not conduct arrests or demand money over WhatsApp/Skype.

8.	App & OS Hygiene

Only download study tools or games from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Sideloading apps from unknown links can install "Trojans" that record your screen and steal your banking credentials.

How to Report Cybercrime

If you have been defrauded or suspect a scam, act immediately to increase your chances of freezing the stolen funds:

•	National Cyber Crime Helpline: Call 1930 immediately.

•	Online Reporting: Lodge a formal complaint at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
